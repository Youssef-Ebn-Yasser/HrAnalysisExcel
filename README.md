HR Attrition Analysis: Executive Summary
Project Overview
This analysis aims to identify key factors contributing to employee attrition. By examining demographic, departmental, and satisfaction data, this documentation provides a roadmap for understanding why employees leave and how to improve retention.
Data Dictionary
The analysis is based on a dataset containing the following key columns:
 * Demographics: Age, Gender, CF_Age_Band, Marital Status, Over 18.
 * Employment Details: Department, Job Role, Job Level, Education Field, Monthly Income, Num Companies Worked.
 * Engagement & Satisfaction: Environment Satisfaction, Job Involvement, Job Satisfaction, Training Last Year.
 * Attrition Metrics: Attrition (Yes/No), CF_Attrition_Label, CF_Attrition_Count, CF_Attrition_Rate.
Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
The following high-level metrics were calculated to provide a snapshot of the current workforce:
| Metric | Description |
|---|---|
| Total Employees | The headcount of all unique Emp No entries in the system. |
| Total Attrition | The sum of employees who have left the company (Attrition = Yes). |
| Active Employees | The count of current staff (Total Employees minus Total Attrition). |
| Attrition Rate | The percentage of the workforce that has left. |
Visual Analysis & Insights
1. Workforce Composition
 * Total Employees by Gender: A breakdown showing the distribution of male and female employees across the organization.
 * Total Employees by Education: Highlights the educational background of the workforce, identifying the most common degrees or levels of study.
2. Attrition Deep-Dive
 * Attrition by Job Role: Identifies which specific positions (e.g., Sales Executive, Research Scientist) experience the highest turnover.
 * Attrition by Age Band: Shows which age groups (e.g., 25–34, 35–44) are most likely to leave, helping target retention programs.
 * Attrition by Marital Status: Analyzes whether single, married, or divorced employees show different patterns of leaving.
3. Productivity & Satisfaction Factors
 * Overtime & Monthly Income: Examines the relationship between heavy workloads (Overtime) and compensation levels in relation to attrition.
 * Satisfaction Scores: Correlates Job Satisfaction and Environment Satisfaction with the CF_Attrition_Count to pinpoint cultural issues.
Conclusion & Recommendations
The analysis suggests that attrition is rarely caused by a single factor. By monitoring the Attrition Rate against Job Satisfaction and Age Bands, HR can develop targeted interventions, such as adjusted compensation for high-risk roles or improved work-life balance for departments with high overtime.
